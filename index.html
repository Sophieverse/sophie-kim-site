About

I’m Sophie — 18, on a gap year before Stanford, and I’ve been building things for as long as I can remember. I founded my first organization at 14 and two more before I graduated high school as valedictorian of a class of 671.

In 2026, I went from no AI safety experience to youngest person ever admitted to the ERA Fellowship in Cambridge in under two months — I turned 18 on the flight there. At ERA, I’m building bioweapon.ai and researching tiered access controls for AI bio capabilities.

I come from a long line of people who left everything behind for a chance at something better. My grandfather boarded a boat to America with a few dollars in his pocket, leaving his wife and two small children behind in rural Korea, because he understood that staying meant stagnation. That urgency is in my blood.

“Intergenerational debt does not end with repayment to those behind. It extends forward, to all who come after.”

There has never been a doubt in my mind that startups are the right path. It’s just a matter of what and when.
